Privacy policy

Barcodehammer — read and create barcodes, offline

About Privacy policy Support Terms of use Language: Nederlands · English · Español

Last updated: 21 July 2026 · Applies to the Barcodehammer app (Android and iOS)

In short: the app works fully offline and does not collect, share or transmit any personal data. No accounts, no advertising, no trackers, no analytics. Everything stays on your own device, and the history is stored there encrypted.

1. What data is collected?

None. The app requests no internet permission and makes no connection. There is no server for anything to arrive at.

Concretely: no registration or credentials, no advertising identifiers, no analytics or crash reporting, no location data, contacts, calendar or microphone, and no cookies.

2. What stays on your device?

The history of what you scanned and created, plus your language and theme choice. The history is stored encrypted with AES-GCM; the key is held in the operating system's secure storage (Android Keystore, iOS Keychain) and never leaves it.

Why that matters: a scanned code can contain anything — a wifi password, a login code, a home address. That does not belong on disk in readable form.

3. Deleting

You can remove individual entries, or the entire history at once after a warning. In the latter case the key is replaced as well, so that anything left of the old file on disk can no longer be read.

Uninstalling the app removes everything it stored.

4. The camera

The camera is used only to read codes. No photos or videos are taken, stored or transmitted; the image is analysed live to recognise a code in it.

5. Sharing with third parties

Nothing is shared, sold or transferred, for the simple reason that nothing is collected and the app has no network connection.

6. Children

The app is suitable for all ages and collects data from no one, children included.

7. Changes

Any significant change — for instance if a feature requiring internet were ever added — will be made clear in the app and on this page before that feature becomes available.
